About General Trias

Discover our rich history and vibrant community

Our History

General Trias, originally named San Francisco de Malabon, was established as a municipality in 1748. The town was renamed in 1920 in honor of General Mariano Trias, a revolutionary leader during the Philippine Revolution against Spain and the first Vice President of the Revolutionary Government.

The city played a significant role during the Philippine Revolution. It was in San Francisco de Malabon where Andres Bonifacio and the Katipunan planned many of their revolutionary activities. The famous "Cry of Cavite" was initiated in this town.

Cityhood

General Trias was converted from a municipality into a component city of Cavite by virtue of Republic Act No. 10675, signed by President Benigno Aquino III on August 19, 2015. The cityhood was ratified through a plebiscite held on December 12, 2015.

Geography

General Trias is located in the northern part of Cavite province, approximately 30 kilometers south of Manila. It has a total land area of 81.46 square kilometers, making it one of the larger cities in Cavite in terms of land area.
